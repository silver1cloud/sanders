Bernie Sanders

Then

  • 1941 - Born in Brooklyn New York
  • 1964 - Graduated from the University of Chicago
  • 1968 - Settled in Vermont
  • 1970s - Ran unsuccessful third-party campaigns for governor and U.S. senator
  • 1981 - Elected mayor of Burlington Vermont
  • 1990 - Elected to represent Vermont's at-large congressional district in the U.S. House of Representatives
  • 2006 - Elected to the U.S. Senate

Now

  • 2015 - Sanders campaigns for the Democratic presidential nomination. His campaign is noted for the enthusiasm of its supporters, as well as his rejection of large donations from corporations, the financial industry, and any Super PAC.
  • 2016 - Sander's book "Our Revolution: A Future to Believe In" is released at number 3 on NYT bestseller list.
  • 2016 - Sanders forms a political organization, Our Revolution, to educate voters, get people involved in the political process, and work to elect progressive candidates.

This is what real leadership looks like.

Both the NAACP and the NHLA have given Sanders 100% voting scores during his tenure in the Senate. In 2015 Sanders was named one of the Top 5 of The Forward 50. In a November 2015 Morning Consult poll, Sanders had an approval rating of 83% among his constituents, making him the most popular senator in the country. Fox News found Sanders to have the highest net favorability at +28 points of any prominent politician included in its March 2017 poll.A Harvard-Harris Poll published April 18, 2017 found Sanders to be the most popular active politician in the country.
